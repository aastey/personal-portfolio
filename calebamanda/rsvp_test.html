Caleb and Amanda

About Caleb and Amanda

There's only one place to go when you're looking for something awesome these days... the internet! In December, 2009 Caleb and Amanda found each other on the oh-so-popular website, OKCupid. Amanda was attracted to Caleb's disregard for the profile questions while Caleb was attracted to Amanda's bold internet persona.

The living arrangements were a little awkward "Oh, you live in that apartment building? I live in this apartment building... across the street..." but the couple found that living so close to each other worked in their favor. A few dates and a snowpocalypse later, the pair was inseparable. They walked, ran, and even rollerbladed to each other's abodes to spend time with their exciting new partners.

In their time together Caleb and Amanda discovered their mutual love of intelligent sci-fi, random weeknight adventures, and kittens (especially those named Mayhem). They did not agree on Joss Whedon and Hockey, but they learned to live with that. They found that they were excellent travel partners and in the past few years they have explored New York City, Pittsburgh, Williamsburg, San Francisco, London, Cambridge, Bucharest, Rome, Florence, and Venice.

Over the past few years Caleb and Amanda have combined their acquaintances and created the best friend group anyone could imagine. They never would have gotten here without the continued love and support from wine night, potlucks, bands, hockey teams, community theater, fencing and flashband. And, of course, they couldn't be more grateful for their close-knit, phenomenal families.

Caleb and Amanda can't wait to take this next step in their relationship and they look forward to a lifetime of their infamous "magic tricks" and late night debates on the living room couch.

The Proposal

Apparently Amanda's voice raises 3 octaves when she's surprised!

When Amanda woke up the morning of Sunday, March 3rd, she didn't notice anything out of the ordinary. The day was pre-planned - Caleb would record with "All the Best Kids" in the basement while Amanda would prepare the house for a potluck that evening. It was decidedly un-extraordinary.

However, when it came time for the potluck... no one showed up. Caleb convinced a confused Amanda to relax with him on the couch. She obliged and leaned her head on his chest. "Why... is your heart beating so fast?" she asked. Caleb had no explanation, but Amanda thought nothing of it.

Suddenly, Caleb popped up from the couch. He walked to the back of the house with great purpose. Amanda followed as her questions of "What are you doing?" were answered with silence. After some fumbling with the back door, Caleb and Amanda stepped onto their porch and it all became clear. Roughly 40 of their closest friends were waiting with music, candles and a sign that read "Will you Marry Me?"

Glen Echo Park

Glen Echo Park is a former amusement park that has been repurposed as a local arts center. It is located on the edge of Potomac, Maryland and has always been one of Amanda's favorite locations in the DC area. The roller coasters and carnival games are gone, but a beautiful space remains. Perhaps its most appealing feature is the beautiful Dentzel carousel, which guests will be able to ride during the cocktail hour!

Ceremony

The wedding ceremony will be held in the Cuddle-Up Pavilion - a covered outdoor space roughly 200 feet from the reception area. It is a cheerful and bright little structure that will bring a fun feel to the event.

Reception

When it comes time to party, guests can easily saunter over to the old bumper-car pavilion for the reception. This covered area is a beautiful space with a wooden stage, removable walls (available in case of rain) and year-round christmas lights. Unfortunately all bumper cars have been removed, but Amanda prefers dance floors to sore necks and crushed fingers .
